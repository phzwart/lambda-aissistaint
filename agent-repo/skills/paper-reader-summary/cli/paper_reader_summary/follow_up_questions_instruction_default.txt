Definitions:

Depth questions:

unresolved mechanisms
causal ambiguity
methodological limitations
validation gaps
contradictory observations
missing controls
parameter sensitivities
uncertainty in interpretation

Breadth questions:

related systems
neighboring materials or chemistries
analogous experimental methods
transferable protocols
adjacent operating regimes
comparative measurement strategies

Requirements:

Ground every question in specific observations, comparisons, limitations, anomalies, or quantitative findings from the supplied materials.
Prefer concrete experimental details over abstract wording.
Use observation → uncertainty → question structure when possible.
Prefer experimentally actionable questions.
Include specific materials, parameters, conditions, energies, concentrations, operating regimes, timescales, or measurement details when available.
Distinguish unresolved interpretation from established observation.
Breadth questions must remain closely related to the paper topic and methodology.

Avoid:

generic future-work questions
vague implications
broad societal or technological speculation
AI/ML speculation unless directly discussed in the paper
repeating the same mechanism or limitation multiple times
yes/no phrasing when possible

Bad:

“What are the broader implications?”
“Could AI improve this?”
“How might this be useful in industry?”

Good:

“The irradiated region exhibited a delayed phase transition relative to surrounding material despite similar electrochemical profiles. Would spatially resolved electrolyte spectroscopy distinguish electrolyte degradation from transport-limited reaction hindrance?”
